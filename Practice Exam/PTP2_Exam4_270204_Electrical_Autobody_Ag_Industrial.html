A – Charging Systems (8 Questions)

Q1. The primary purpose of the charging system on a vehicle or machine is to:

  1. Reduce fuel consumption only
  2. Recharge the battery and supply current for electrical loads during operation
  3. Control engine oil pressure
  4. Regulate fuel injector timing
Show answer

Correct answer: b) Recharge the battery and supply current for electrical loads during operation

Q2. The main components of a typical charging system are:

  1. Starter, battery cables and flywheel
  2. Battery, alternator, voltage regulator, wiring harness and electrical loads
  3. Battery only
  4. Alternator, fuel pump and spark plugs
Show answer

Correct answer: b) Battery, alternator, voltage regulator, wiring harness and electrical loads

Q3. The alternator brushes make contact with which component to supply current to the rotor field winding?

  1. Commutator bars
  2. Slip rings
  3. Stator laminations
  4. Rectifier diodes
Show answer

Correct answer: b) Slip rings

Q4. Alternator output current is produced when:

  1. The stator is magnetized by permanent magnets alone
  2. The rotor’s rotating magnetic field cuts through the stator windings
  3. The battery discharges through the starter motor
  4. The voltage regulator opens all circuits
Show answer

Correct answer: b) The rotor’s rotating magnetic field cuts through the stator windings

Q5. A stronger magnetic field in the alternator rotor will:

  1. Reduce alternator output amperage
  2. Increase alternator output amperage for a given speed
  3. Have no effect on alternator output
  4. Stop alternator rotation
Show answer

Correct answer: b) Increase alternator output amperage for a given speed

Q6. When supplying a replacement alternator, you should compare the new unit with the old one to ensure:

  1. Pulley and fan, mounting points and electrical connections match the original
  2. Colour of the alternator housing is identical
  3. Battery size is unchanged
  4. Spark plug gap is correct
Show answer

Correct answer: a) Pulley and fan, mounting points and electrical connections match the original

Q7. The voltage regulator in a charging system is responsible for:

  1. Controlling fuel pressure
  2. Limiting the charging system voltage to protect the battery and electrical components
  3. Adjusting ignition timing
  4. Controlling engine coolant flow
Show answer

Correct answer: b) Limiting the charging system voltage to protect the battery and electrical components

Q8. An undercharged battery on a machine with a functioning alternator could be caused by:

  1. A slipping alternator drive belt
  2. Overinflated tires
  3. Incorrect engine oil viscosity
  4. Clean battery terminals

Correct answer: a) A slipping alternator drive belt
