A – Hydraulic Fundamentals (9 Questions)

Q1. Hydrodynamics in a hydraulic system is best described as:

  1. Transmission of force using confined but stationary liquid
  2. Transmission of force or motion through the flow of liquids
  3. Transmission of force through a solid lever system
  4. Transmission of force using compressed air only
Show answer

Correct answer: b) Transmission of force or motion through the flow of liquids

Q2. Hydrostatics refers to:

  1. Force transmission using rapidly moving liquid
  2. Force transmission using confined liquids that may be stationary
  3. Force transmission using compressed gases
  4. Force transmission using mechanical linkages only
Show answer

Correct answer: b) Force transmission using confined liquids that may be stationary

Q3. An example of a primarily hydrostatic application would be a:

  1. Hydraulic motor driving a conveyor
  2. Hydraulic steering system using continuous flow through an orbitrol
  3. Bottle jack supporting a lifted vehicle with the pump stopped
  4. Hydraulic cooling fan that runs at variable speed
Show answer

Correct answer: c) Bottle jack supporting a lifted vehicle with the pump stopped

Q4. Pascal’s law states that pressure applied to a confined fluid:

  1. Is greatest at the pump outlet and lowest at the actuator
  2. Acts only in the direction of flow
  3. Is transmitted equally in all directions throughout the fluid
  4. Is cancelled by gravity at the reservoir
Show answer

Correct answer: c) Is transmitted equally in all directions throughout the fluid

Q5. A hydraulic press uses a 2 cm² input piston and a 20 cm² output piston. Ignoring losses, the mechanical advantage in force is approximately:

  1. 1:1
  2. 2:1
  3. 10:1
  4. 20:1
Show answer

Correct answer: c) 10:1

Q6. To increase mechanical advantage in a basic hydraulic jack without changing pressure, you would:

  1. Decrease the area of the load piston
  2. Increase the area of the load piston
  3. Increase reservoir size only
  4. Decrease oil viscosity only
Show answer

Correct answer: b) Increase the area of the load piston

Q7. When storing hydraulic fluid, a key precaution is to:

  1. Store open drums outdoors to allow moisture to vent
  2. Leave containers unsealed to equalize pressure
  3. Protect fluids from contamination, separation, oxidation and freezing
  4. Mix different grades to reduce inventory
Show answer

Correct answer: c) Protect fluids from contamination, separation, oxidation and freezing

Q8. A hydraulic system relying on fast oil flow through a motor to create rotation is using:

  1. Mainly hydrostatic principles
  2. Mainly hydrodynamic principles
  3. Only mechanical principles
  4. Pneumatic principles
Show answer

Correct answer: b) Mainly hydrodynamic principles

Q9. Which condition is most likely to reduce the effectiveness of a hydraulic system based on Pascal’s law?

  1. Trapped air compressibility in the circuit
  2. Use of liquid with higher viscosity
  3. Increasing fluid temperature to normal range
  4. Using steel instead of aluminum for lines

Correct answer: a) Trapped air compressibility in the circuit
