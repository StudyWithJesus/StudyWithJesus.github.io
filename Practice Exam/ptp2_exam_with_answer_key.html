    Horizontally opposed – one crankshaft in the center and pistons move outward 180° apart.

4.  b) Fuel and ignition systems.

270201b Engine Blocks and Liners

2.  c) Water jacket.
3.  a) Is supported by block material from top to bottom.
4.  b) Integral liners.
5.  d) Wet liner.


270201c Pistons, Piston Rings and Connecting Rods

1.  Pin passes through the piston pin bosses and connecting rod eyelet end. Pin can rotate in both and is retained by snap rings.
2.  Allows installation/removal of ring from piston and allows for heat expansion.
3.  Piston skirt manufactured in an oval (elliptical) shape, longer across pin bosses; becomes round as it warms. Also called elliptical or oval piston.
4.  a) Cast aluminum.
5.  a) Connecting rod.


270201d Crankshafts and Related Parts

1.  Casting – cast crankshaft; Forging – forged crankshaft.
2.  By oil passageways drilled through the crankshaft.
3.  Harmonic balancer (vibration dampener) absorbs torsional vibration and assists in balancing crankshaft (external balanced engines). Flywheel maintains crankshaft rotation between power strokes and assists in balancing (external balanced engines).
4.  Two.
5.  a) Connecting rod bearings.
6.  Main bearing with thrust faces or separate thrust washers.


270201e Camshafts and Related Parts

1.  Located in camshaft bore in block or cylinder head; pressed in with interference fit. Often different sizes and marked for location.
2.  Roller lifter.
3.  d) Tappet, timing chain, shell.
4.  c) Valve clearance.
5.  d) Parkerizing.
6.  Camshaft rotates at ½ the speed of the crankshaft.


270201f Cylinder Heads and Related Parts

1.  Valves are in the cylinder head; arrangement called overhead valve (OHV) used on in-line or V-style engines.
2.  Holds the valve in alignment so valve face and seat make proper contact. Types: integral and replaceable.
3.  Cylinder head bolts (cap screws); studs and nuts.
4.  d) Valve rotator.
5.  a) Larger than that of an exhaust valve.
6.  b) A valve seal kit (top end kit).


270201g Engine Cooling Systems

1.  Bring engine up to operating temperature quickly; remove surplus heat; maintain efficient operating temperature.
2.  Thermostat regulates engine temperature; high temperature thermostats help engine warm up quickly and run warmer, reducing emissions.
3.  Ethylene glycol – for gasoline, LPG & NG engines, protects aluminum/alloy components.
    Propylene glycol – for diesel; better cavitation and corrosion protection, low silicates, doesn’t plug cores, less toxic.
4.  Boiling point increases: each 1 psi raises boiling point by about 3.25°F. 8 psi × 3.25°F ≈ 26°F increase.
5.  Prevent hose from collapsing when engine cools.


270201h Engine Lubrication Systems

1.  Lubricate, seal, cushion, clean, cool.
2.  SAE rates engine oils by viscosity (fluid’s resistance to flow).
3.  Pleated paper and non-woven inorganic media. Ability rated by depth, surface, and Beta rating.
4.  API rates service rating by amounts and types of additives. (Current categories given in latest standards.)
5.  Full flow filtration; partial flow or by-pass filtration.


270201i Exhaust Systems

1.  Steel or cast iron.
2.  In exhaust manifold, header pipe, before and after catalytic converter, or elsewhere in exhaust system.
3.  To quiet the exhaust; muffler and resonator names often used interchangeably.
4.  Prevent transfer of exhaust system vibration to vehicle body; allow for expansion due to heat.
5.  c) Hangers, clamps, and a pipe adapter.


270201j Air Induction Systems

1.  Filters out larger debris such as straw and leaves.
2.  Causes incoming air to swirl so dirt is thrown against housing and removed.
3.  MAP – Manifold Absolute Pressure; senses pressure in intake manifold and sends signal to ECM.
4.  Driven by engine exhaust; exhaust turbine drives compressor impeller that forces air into engine.
5.  Turbocharger must be sealed and packaged to prevent rotation of turbine and impeller.


270201k Fuel Properties and Supply Systems

1.  Octane rating is gasoline’s ability to resist pre-ignition and detonation; also relates to speed of burn.
2.  a) Diesel.
3.  EVAP system – domed fuel tank, fuel separator, tank filler cap with pressure/vacuum valve, charcoal canister, purge valve.
4.  Electric pump mounted inside fuel tank.
5.  Fuel tank module / fuel cluster.


270201l Gasoline Fuel Injection Systems

1.  Fuel injector – delivers fine spray of fuel that vaporizes at the correct time.
2.  Maintains equal pressure at injectors and increases pressure under wide open throttle.
3.  ECM varies injector pulse width; longer pulse = more fuel.
4.  ECM monitors oxygen sensor signal in closed loop.
5.  Top feed and bottom feed injectors.
6.  Fuel pump mounted inside fuel tank, often as part of fuel module (sending unit + pump).
